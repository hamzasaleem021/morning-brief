Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 27, 2026

Morning Brief is designed with your privacy in mind. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your information.

What We Collect

Analytics Data (Amplitude)

We use Amplitude Analytics to understand how people use Morning Brief. This helps us improve the app and fix bugs. We collect:

  • Anonymous device identifiers — A random ID assigned to your browser (not your personal information)
  • Feature usage — Which buttons you click, sources you add/remove, how often you refresh
  • Session information — When you open the app, how long you use it
  • Technical data — Browser type, device type, screen size

We do NOT collect your IP address or any personally identifiable information through analytics.

Account Data (Google OAuth & Supabase)

If you choose to enable cloud sync, we collect:

  • Email address — Used only for authentication and magic link login
  • Google profile info — Name and profile picture (if signing in with Google)
  • Your RSS sources — The list of RSS feeds you've added (stored encrypted in Supabase)

What We Don't Collect

  • Your RSS feed URLs — Not shared with analytics (only stored for your sync)
  • Article content — We never store or analyze what you read
  • Browsing history — We only track activity within Morning Brief
  • Location data — No GPS or precise location tracking
  • Social media activity — We don't track what you do outside this app

How We Use Data

Analytics Data

Used to:

  • Understand which features are popular and improve them
  • Identify and fix bugs
  • See how many people use the app daily
  • Make data-driven decisions about new features

All analytics data is aggregated and anonymous. We can't identify individual users from this data.

Account & Sync Data

Used to:

  • Authenticate your account via magic link or Google OAuth
  • Sync your RSS sources across all your devices
  • Enable real-time updates when you add/remove sources

Third-Party Services

Analytics platform for anonymous usage tracking.
Privacy policy: amplitude.com/privacy

Supabase

Backend database for account authentication and cloud sync.
Privacy policy: supabase.com/privacy

Google OAuth

Sign-in service (optional, only if you use cloud sync).

Cloudflare Workers

Data Storage & Security

Local Storage

Your RSS sources and app preferences are stored in your browser's local storage. This data never leaves your device unless you enable cloud sync.

Cloud Sync (Optional)

If you sign in with email or Google, your RSS sources are encrypted and stored in Supabase. This allows you to access your sources from multiple devices.

Data Retention

  • Analytics — Retained for 1 year (Amplitude free tier)
  • Account data — Retained until you delete your account
  • Local data — Stored until you clear browser data

Your Rights

Opt Out of Analytics

You can disable analytics tracking at any time. Once disabled, no usage data will be sent to Amplitude.

Note: This feature will be added to the app settings soon.

Delete Your Account Data

To delete your synced data:

  1. Sign out of your account in the app
  2. Clear your browser's local storage
  3. Email us to request deletion of server-side data

Access Your Data

You can view all your synced RSS sources in the "Manage Sources" section of the app. To request a copy of all data we have about you, contact us via email.

Cookies

Morning Brief uses minimal cookies:

  • Amplitude cookie — Stores your anonymous device ID for analytics
  • Supabase session cookie — Keeps you logged in (if using cloud sync)
  • Local storage — Stores your RSS sources and theme preference

No third-party advertising or tracking cookies are used.

Children's Privacy

Morning Brief is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. Continued use of Morning Brief after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
